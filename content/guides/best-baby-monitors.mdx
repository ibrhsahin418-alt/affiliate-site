## Types of Baby Monitors

Before diving into specific models, it's important to understand the three main types:

**1. Dedicated (Non-WiFi) Monitors**
- Operate on their own closed-frequency radio signal
- No internet connection — cannot be hacked remotely
- Range-limited to the home
- No smartphone access

**2. WiFi / Cloud-Connected Monitors**
- Stream video to a smartphone app
- Accessible anywhere with internet
- Security depends on the manufacturer's encryption and practices
- Often include AI features (motion detection, crying alerts)

**3. Wearable Baby Monitors**
- Clip onto diaper or fit into sock/band
- Track breathing rate, oxygen levels, and movement
- Medical-grade models are expensive; consumer models vary in accuracy

## Buying Advice

**Security tip:** If you choose a WiFi monitor, check whether the manufacturer publishes security audits. Change the default password immediately. Enable two-factor authentication if available.

**Range tip:** Manufacturers test range in open air. In a typical two-story home with thick walls, expect 40–60% of the stated range. If your nursery is far from your bedroom, choose a model with a stronger signal.

**Budget tip:** The monitor itself is only part of the cost. WiFi monitors with subscription features (Nanit, Owlet) add $100–$200/year in ongoing costs. Factor this in when comparing prices.
