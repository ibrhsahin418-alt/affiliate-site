## Blade Quality and Edge Retention

Victorinox uses **high-carbon stainless steel** with a proprietary hardening process that achieves a Rockwell hardness of approximately **56 HRC**. This is:

- **Softer than Japanese steel** (typically 58–66 HRC) — meaning it won't hold an edge quite as long
- **Tougher than Japanese steel** — meaning it's far more resistant to chipping if it hits a bone or hard surface

The practical result: you'll sharpen these knives more often than a Japanese knife, but they'll forgive the occasional misuse that would chip a harder blade.

## Maintenance Tips

1. **Hand wash** — dishwasher safe per specs, but detergent dulls edges faster
2. **Hone weekly** with a honing rod to maintain alignment
3. **Sharpen every 3–6 months** depending on use frequency
4. **Store in the block** — knife-on-knife contact damages edges
